On Paper · series playbook · v1

How an On Paper essay gets made.

One repeatable route from “next topic” to published essay: the format, the research method, the build pipeline, and the rules that keep the series one thing. Water was the first run; oil is next.

§1 · The format

One essay, one scroll.

The current format is the right one. Each topic is a linear argument — promise, gap, cost, lever — and a scroll is the reading motion for a linear argument. A conventional multi-page website would break that spine into navigation. The essay format is the series identity.

The essayOne scrollytelling page per resource — “Water on Paper,” “Oil on Paper.” This is the product.
The hubA thin index page listing the essays. Build it once there are two — not before.
The companionAn interactive spin-off (Source to Tap) — only when a topic earns a tool too big for one chapter.

§2 · The arc

Eight chapters, fixed.

Every essay walks the same arc. The topic changes what fills each chapter; the arc itself does not move.

01The scale

The global picture as one proportion. Water: 100 drops, 3 fresh, 1 reachable.

02On paper

The promise vs. the delivery, side by side. The right to water vs. the reach of the tap.

03The line

Who is on the wrong side of it, ranked. Ten most water-stressed countries.

04India — the centrepiece

The state-by-state signature visual. The periodic-table cartogram.

05The wound

One named place where the failure has a face. Indore.

06The tool

“Know your X,” interactive. Neer.

07The reckoning

The cost you are complicit in — the lens turned on yourself. AI’s water bill.

08The levers

Every issue raised exits with a solution. Four actions.

Rhythm: paper base throughout · one dark chapter (03/07) · the wound in the alarm colour · never more than two background colours.

§3 · The research method

Five layers, in order — and one rule.

To truly understand a resource, work the funnel top-down. Each layer has its own question, its own sources, and feeds specific chapters. Don’t start writing until every layer has numbers.

1Global
How much exists, who has it, where is the access line?
Sources: UN agencies (WHO/UNICEF, FAO — for oil: IEA, EIA, OPEC), World Bank, Our World in Data.
Feeds: the Ch 01 proportion · three global numbers · the ten-country ranking.
2India, on paper
What is promised — laws, targets, schemes, prices?
Sources: the ministry itself (Jal Shakti — for oil: MoPNG, PPAC), NITI Aayog, CAG audits.
Feeds: the “what the page says” column of Ch 02.
3India, in reality
What is actually delivered, state by state?
Sources: official statistics read against independent studies and reporting.
Feeds: the state dataset behind the Ch 04 signature visual and the tool.
4The human layer
One named place, dated events, real people.
Sources: local reporting, court records, RTI responses.
Feeds: Ch 05 — the wound.
5Impact & levers
What does the gap cost — and what closes it?
Sources: peer-reviewed impact studies; organisations already doing the work, with URLs.
Feeds: Ch 07–08. Measure impact on three axes: health, economy, ecology.

The rules

  • →No issue without a lever. Every problem named must exit with a viable solution: a policy, a technology, an org already doing it, and one thing a reader can do.
  • →Triangulate every figure across ≥2 independent sources; record the year and the definition (“access” ≠ “safely managed”).
  • →data.js is the single source of truth. No number lives only in prose or markup.
  • →Log SOURCES[] from day one, not at the end.
  • →Label every figure measured / estimated / claimed — and say so in copy when it matters.
  • →Keep the honesty chapter. Ch 07 turns the lens on yourself; it is what buys the reader’s trust.

§4 · The pipeline

Per topic, seven phases.

1ResearchFill the five-layer funnel (§3). Build data.js as you read: figures, country list, state dataset, scale, sources.1–2 weeks
2OutlineMap the eight chapters. Write the pull quotes first — they are the spine. One idea, one number, one visual per chapter.1–2 days
3Signature visualInvent one new visualisation for Ch 04. Everything else reuses the kit: dot grid, bars, count-ups, two-up cards.2–3 days
4BuildReuse the shell — tokens, type, grain, scroll engine, progress bar, drawer. Swap in the accent, the data, the signature visual.3–5 days
5VerifyFact-check every rendered number against data.js against source. Read all copy aloud. Test at phone width.1 day
6ShipEssay + print variant. Companion tool only if a chapter outgrew the page. Add the essay to the hub.½ day

§5 · Series identity

What stays, what changes.

Constant — the series

  • Paper & ink palette, the grain
  • Instrument Serif / Spline Sans / Spline Sans Mono
  • The eight-chapter arc
  • Mono kickers, sup-style citations
  • Progress bar, chapter drawer
  • “X on Paper” naming
  • data.js discipline

Variable — the topic

  • The accent pair (water blue + clay → oil’s own)
  • What the five-stop stress scale measures
  • The Ch 04 signature visual
  • The interactive tool
  • The wound — the case study
  • The reckoning — your own complicity in it

§6 · The roadmap

Six essays make a volume.

Think in volumes of six, not one endless series — ship the set, then greenlight the next. A topic earns its place only by clearing all five gates below. Water is 1 of 6; here is the rest of Volume I.

The gate · five tests

1A substanceIt can sit on a page. Water, oil, ink — the title pun is the identity. “Healthcare” fails; blood passes.
2A proportionThere is a “100 drops” scale to open on — 100 breaths, 100 grains, 100 rupees.
3A promise on paperA right, law, scheme or ministry guarantees it — the thesis engine: on paper X is ___, in reality ___.
4A state-by-state splitReal variance for the Ch 04 centrepiece. India is the centrepiece — no split, no essay.
5A wound & a reckoningOne named place where it failed, and one way the reader is complicit.

Oil on Paper — starter sheet.

Thesis candidate: “On paper, oil is a price at the pump. In reality it is a dependency priced into everything you eat, breathe and buy.”

Global questionsIf the world’s daily oil were 100 barrels — who pumps them, who burns them? Where is the line between energy-secure and energy-poor?
India, on paperWhat do pricing formulas, ethanol-blending targets and strategic-reserve goals promise? (MoPNG, PPAC, NITI Aayog.)
India, in realityImport share of crude · days of strategic-reserve cover · the tax anatomy of ₹100 at the pump · state-by-state price spread.
Wound candidatesBaghjan blowout (Assam, 2020) · Ennore oil spill (Chennai, 2017) · Delhi’s winter diesel air. Pick one; verify everything fresh.
Lever candidatesEthanol blending · EVs and freight rail · public transit · refinery efficiency — which are real at scale, and who is already doing them?
Signature-visual ideasThe barrel dissected into refinery cuts · the anatomy of the pump price · India’s crude drawn as sea lanes and refineries.
Accent candidatesBitumen black + deep amber, on the same paper base.

This sheet lists questions, not facts — every number gets sourced at Phase 1, never carried over from memory.
